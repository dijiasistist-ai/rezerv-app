# Tyee Product Blueprint

Tyee should not position itself as a simple commission marketplace.

The stronger model is:

- customer acquisition through the marketplace
- operational ownership through the business panel
- customer memory through CRM
- retention through campaigns
- financial clarity through revenue / expense / profit tracking

## Product Pillars

1. Marketplace
- discovery
- search
- reservation flow
- payments

2. Business OS
- calendar
- services
- staff / room / field capacity
- subscriptions
- walk-in and manual bookings

3. CRM
- customer card
- visit history
- spending history
- notes
- consent-based campaign eligibility

4. Finance
- deposits
- venue collection
- expenses
- payouts
- daily cash summary
- service-level profitability

5. Admin Control Tower
- onboarding quality
- business health
- revenue monitoring
- fraud / abuse controls
- support operations

## What The Customer Sees

- premium storefront
- clean service list
- transparent payment logic
- business trust signals
- date / time / service summary

## What The Business Sees

- today dashboard
- customer memory
- occupancy and channel mix
- campaigns and reactivation
- net revenue and cost visibility

## What Admin Sees

- GMV and net revenue
- active businesses and onboarding queue
- support workload
- quality and trust metrics
- risky accounts and payment exceptions

## MVP Priority

1. Reservation + calendar
2. Customer card + reservation history
3. Deposit / venue balance / payout visibility
4. Expense entry + simple profit summary
5. Consent-aware CRM campaigns

## Differentiation

- Turkey-first flows
- startup speed and simplicity
- Fresha-like operational depth
- AI-powered visual storefront improvements
- AI-assisted reactivation and growth suggestions
